About

A New Mexico builder, end to end.

Straight A Builders is a family-run residential construction company serving northern and central New Mexico. We design and build homes that respect the land, the climate, and the way our clients actually live.

From foundation to finish carpentry, every project is led by a senior builder on site — no handoffs, no surprises. Just straight lines, square corners, and houses that hold up.

  • Licensed & insured general contractor
  • In-house framing, finish, and project management
  • Trusted local subcontractor network
  • Transparent budgets and fixed-bid options

FAQ

Frequently asked questions.

Everything to know before we break ground in New Mexico.

Where in New Mexico does Straight A Builders work?

We build across central and northern New Mexico — Albuquerque, Rio Rancho, Santa Fe, Española, and Taos — including outlying neighborhoods within each region.

What types of homes do you build?

We focus on residential general contracting: ground-up custom homes (modern farmhouses, Spanish-style stucco, and estate-scale residences), additions, whole-home remodels, kitchens and baths, and outdoor living spaces.

Are you licensed and insured in New Mexico?

Yes. Straight A Builders is a licensed and insured general contractor in the state of New Mexico. We carry general liability and workers' compensation coverage on every project.

How long does a custom home take to build in New Mexico?

Most custom homes take 9 to 14 months from groundbreaking to move-in, depending on size, finishes, and permitting timelines. Pre-construction (design, budgeting, and permits) typically adds 2 to 4 months before that.

Do you offer fixed-bid pricing or only cost-plus?

We offer both. Most clients choose a fixed-bid contract once design and selections are finalized, but we'll structure cost-plus or hybrid arrangements when that fits the project better.

Do you handle permitting and design coordination?

Yes. Our pre-construction service includes site evaluation, partnership with your architect or designer, full permitting through the relevant New Mexico jurisdiction, and detailed budgeting before construction begins.
